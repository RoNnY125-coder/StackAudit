## Interview 1 — u/the-lost-soul, Independent Developer, Solo
Date: 2026-05-09
Duration: ~10 minutes
Platform: Reddit DM

**Background:**
Solo developer, previously had a small team. Currently pays for Cursor and Claude Pro. Uses both tools daily for development work but admits usage is inconsistent — some weeks he's in them constantly, other weeks barely at all.

**Direct Quotes:**
- "I used to need this for the whole team but now it's just me"
- "Sometimes I go weeks without really using it properly"
- "I just keep paying because switching feels like effort"

**Most Surprising Thing They Said:**
He knew he was probably overpaying — Claude Pro at $20/month and Cursor Pro at $20/month for a single person with inconsistent usage — but hadn't cancelled either because cancellation itself felt like more work than just letting the invoice run. The barrier wasn't cost awareness, it was switching friction.

**What It Changed About the Design:**
The audit results need a direct link next to each recommendation pointing to the plan downgrade page for that tool. Telling someone they're overpaying is not enough if acting on it still feels like effort. Also identified a specific user segment — solo developers who downsized from a team and are still paying team-era prices.

## Interview 2 — A.D., Co-Founder, Early Stage Startup
Date: 2026-05-10
Duration: ~12 minutes
Platform: LinkedIn DM

**Background:**
Co-founder of a small startup building a job search and resume optimization platform
with ATS score checking. Small team, early stage. Using AI tools across coding,
brainstorming, and product management simultaneously.

**Direct Quotes:**
- "OpenAI is way cheaper so it costs us 5-6 dollars"
- "Claude pro for 20 dollars and Gemini I think around 200rs"
- "For Claude? yes, because it is nowadays providing less credits. For others? no,
  we are not overpaying for those."

**Most Surprising Thing They Said:**
He knew Claude was giving less value for money but was still paying for it. The
frustration wasn't the price — it was the credit reduction. He felt the product
got worse while the price stayed the same. This is a different kind of overspend
than I expected — not wrong plan, but wrong tool for the current credit limits.

**What It Changed About the Design:**
The audit engine should flag when a tool has recently reduced its free credits or
changed its value proposition — not just when the plan is wrong for team size.
Also confirmed that small Indian startups are paying in INR for some tools (Gemini
at 200rs) which means the dollar-denominated savings calculations need a note
about currency. Added this to the backlog.
