# Economics

## What a Lead Is Worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI credits. A typical customer spends $1,000/month on AI tools. Credex gets them the same credits at 20-30% off. Margin per customer per purchase is roughly $200-300.

Customers don't buy once. Conservatively assume 3 purchases over 18 months.

Revenue per customer: $250 average margin × 3 purchases = $750 LTV.

Everything else gets measured against that number.

## CAC by Channel

Hacker News Show HN: $0 cash, 2-3 hours to write. If it hits front page that's 2,000+ audits in a day. Even at 1% email capture and 5% booking a consultation, CAC is effectively zero.

Reddit organic: $0. Lower volume than HN but higher intent. People on r/SaaS are actively thinking about costs.

LinkedIn DMs: $0 cash. Figure 2 hours to send 30 personalized messages, 5 replies, 1 conversion. CAC around $60 if time is valued at $30/hr. Fine against $750 LTV.

Newsletter sponsorship: $300-500 per send. If it converts 2 customers, CAC is $150-250. Acceptable.

Credex existing customer email: near zero cost, highest conversion rate of any channel because it is a warm audience that already trusts Credex.

Blended CAC estimate for first 6 months on organic only: $15-30.

## Conversion Math

Start with 1,000 audits completed.

20% capture email = 200 leads. Value is shown before the ask so conversion is higher than a cold form.

Of those 200, the ones showing over $500 in savings see the Credex CTA prominently = roughly 60 people.

20% of those book a consultation = 12 consultations.

30% close rate = 4 customers.

4 customers × $750 LTV = $3,000 revenue from 1,000 audits.

CAC = $3,000 divided by 4 = $750. That is right at LTV which is not good enough yet.

To make the economics work properly: either increase the close rate on consultations through better sales, increase average deal size as customers buy larger credit packages, or reduce CAC by finding better distribution channels.

The lever that moves fastest is distribution. One viral tweet showing real savings numbers can drive 5,000 audits in 48 hours and change the math completely.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months

$1M ARR means $83,333 per month revenue.

At $250 margin per purchase and customers buying twice per year, that requires 332 active purchasing customers.

Month 1-3: Getting the first 20 customers. Mostly from direct outreach and HN post. Revenue is near zero, this is data collection phase.

Month 4-9: Product Hunt launch, newsletter features, word of mouth from shareable audit URLs. Target 80 customers. $20,000 per month run rate.

Month 10-18: SEO from pricing comparison content starts kicking in. Credex existing customer email campaigns. Referral loop from shared audit URLs compounds. Target 332 customers. $83,000 per month.

## What Has to Be True

The shareable URL needs to drive organic signups. If people share their audit results and 10% of viewers run their own audit, growth compounds without paid acquisition. That is the entire growth model. Without virality from the share feature, hitting $1M ARR requires paid acquisition which kills the unit economics at this margin.

The number that tells me the model is working: shareable URL click-through rate. If more than 15% of completed audits generate a link that gets at least one click, the viral loop is real and the rest follows.
