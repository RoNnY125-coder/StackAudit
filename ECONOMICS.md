# Economics

## What a Lead Is Worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI credits. A typical customer is spending $1,000/month on AI tools. Credex can get them the same credits at 20-30% off. That means Credex is capturing maybe $200-300 margin on a $1,000/month customer per purchase cycle.

Customers don't buy once. If the discount is real and the experience is good, they come back. Conservatively assume a customer buys credits 3 times over 18 months.

Revenue per customer: $250 average margin × 3 purchases = $750 LTV.

That's the number everything else gets measured against.

## CAC by Channel

Hacker News post: $0 cash, maybe 3 hours of time. If it hits front page, 2,000+ audits in a day. Even at 1% email capture and 5% of those booking consultations, that's 1 consultation from 2,000 audits — CAC of basically nothing.

Reddit posts: $0. Lower volume but higher intent. r/SaaS readers are actively thinking about this stuff.

LinkedIn DMs: $0 cash. Figure 2 hours to send 30 personalized messages, 5 replies, 1 conversion. If my time is worth $30/hr, CAC is $60. Still fine against $750 LTV.

Newsletter sponsorship: $300-500 per send. If it converts 2 customers, CAC is $150-250. Acceptable.

Credex customer email: near zero cost. Highest conversion rate of any channel because it's a warm audience that already trusts Credex.

## Conversion Math

Run 1,000 audits.
20% capture email = 200 leads.
Of those, audits showing >$500 savings get the Credex CTA = maybe 60 people.
20% of those book a consultation = 12 consultations.
30% close = 4 customers.
4 × $750 LTV = $3,000 from 1,000 audits.

CAC = $3,000 / 4 = $750. That's right at LTV. Not good enough yet.

To make it work: either increase the close rate on consultations (better sales), increase the average deal size (customers buying more credits), or reduce CAC by finding better distribution.

The lever that moves fastest is distribution. One viral tweet showing real savings numbers can drive 5,000 audits in 48 hours and change the math completely.

## Path to $1M ARR

$1M ARR means $83k/month. At $250 margin per purchase and customers buying 2x/year, that's 332 active customers.

Month 1-3: Getting the first 20 customers. Mostly from direct outreach and HN. Revenue: basically zero, data collection phase.

Month 4-9: Product Hunt, newsletter features, word of mouth from shareable URLs. Target 80 customers. Revenue: $20k/month run rate.

Month 10-18: SEO from the pricing comparison content, Credex existing customer emails, referral loop from shared audit URLs. Target 332 customers. Revenue: $83k/month.

What has to be true: The shareable URL needs to drive organic signups. If people share their audit results and 10% of viewers run their own audit, growth compounds. That's the entire growth model. Without virality from the share feature, hitting $1M ARR requires paid acquisition which kills the unit economics.

The number that tells me it's working: shareable URL click-through rate. If >15% of completed audits generate a link that gets clicked, the viral loop is real.
